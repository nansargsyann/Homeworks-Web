The World of Yesterday, mailed to his publisher a few days before Stefan Zweig took his life in 1942, has become a classic of the memoir genre. The Murder on The Links

The Murder on the Links is the third book of detective fiction by Agatha Christie and one of her most famous works. The novel was first published in 1923, in the UK. An urgent letter from a wealthy man, named Paul Renauld, brings Hercule Poirot to France. But on his arrival, the renowned detective finds that his client is dead and his brutally stabbed body now lies face downwards in a shallow grave on a golf course.

The Rebel

"The Rebel" philosophical essay is an attempt to discover the essence of man, which is played out in the focus of the encounter of eternity and historical time, in the turning points of the socio-political upheavals, civilizations and epochs of earthly humanity. This essay is also a desperate cry against the pessimism of the future of mankind. The ideas mentioned in it are in line with the concerns of his great predecessors and contemporaries, why not also the successors.
